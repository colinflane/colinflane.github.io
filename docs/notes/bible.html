Pharisees ('Separatists')
Emerged out of groups of scribes and sages ('rabbis')
Opposed Hellenization of Judaism (Sadducees)
Maintainers of Mosaic authority in form of oral [interpretation of] Torah
Believed in maintaining purity laws even outside Temple
Estimated by Josephus at 6,000 strong
Foundation of Rabbinical Judaism following destruction of Second Temple
Believed the sacred could exist outside the Temple
More widely accepted by common people
Sadducees
Elites of Jewish society, involved in Temple affairs
Descendants of Zadok, of the priestly class
Did not believe in an afterlife or resurrection
Opposed oral Torah (Pharisees)
Reputation for corruption
Extinguished with destruction of Second Temple
Samaritans
Abrahamic religious and ethnic group descended from ancient Israelites, alongside Jews
Believe only in written Torah, not oral Torah or Talmud
Believe God's place is Mount Gerizim, not Temple Mount
High priest Eli split the Israelites after building of first altar in promised land those who followed him, first to Shiloh, would become Jews; those who remained, Samaritans
Often persecuted by Jews
Herodians
Hellenistic Jews opposed to Pharisees and Sadducees
Supporters of Herod the Great, his successors, and efforts to restore their dynasty
